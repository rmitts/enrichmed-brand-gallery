    A structured clinical approach is vital for assessing a patient with a pulmonary nodule. This section outlines the critical questions to ask during history taking and provides a comprehensive, categorized list of differential diagnoses, separating the far more common benign causes from the critical malignant possibilities.

    A Detailed History is Vital

      Clinical Pearl: Compare with old imaging. A solid or subsolid nodule unchanged for >2 years is very likely benign. Nodules in upper lobes are statistically more likely malignant.
